Football

Famous Players:

Lionel Messi

Widely regarded as one of the greatest footballers of all time, Messi's exceptional dribbling skills, vision, and goal-scoring prowess have earned him numerous accolades, including multiple Ballon d'Or awards.

Cristiano Ronaldo

Another legend of the game, Ronaldo's athleticism, and goal-scoring ability have made him a dominant force on the field. Known for his powerful shots and aerial prowess, he has also won multiple Ballon d'Or awards and holds numerous records.

Pele

Regarded by many as the greatest footballer of all time, Pele's skill, creativity, and goal-scoring ability mesmerized audiences during his illustrious career. The Brazilian icon won three FIFA World Cups and remains a symbol of football excellence.

Diego Maradona

Famous for his 'Hand of God' goal and the 'Goal of the Century' in the 1986 FIFA World Cup, Maradona's exceptional dribbling skills and footballing intelligence made him a legend of the game.

Zinedine Zidane

Known for his elegance on the field and incredible technique, Zidane won numerous titles with clubs like Juventus and Real Madrid. His iconic performances in the 1998 FIFA World Cup and the 2002 UEFA Champions League final cemented his status as one of the greatest players in football history.

Basic Rules:

Objective: The primary objective is to score goals by getting the ball into the opposing team's net.

Team Composition: Each team consists of 11 players, including a goalkeeper.

Offside Rule: A player is in an offside position if they are nearer to the opponent's goal line than both the ball and the second-last opponent when the ball is played to them.

Fouls: Various fouls can occur during play, such as tripping, pushing, or handball. The team fouled is typically awarded a free-kick or a penalty kick, depending on the severity and location of the foul.

Duration: A standard football match consists of two halves, each lasting 45 minutes, with a halftime break and in somecases extra time with two 15 minute halves and then penalties.

Substitutions: Coaches can substitute players in and out of the game at any stoppage of play.
