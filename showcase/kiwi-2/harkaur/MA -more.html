Bad Bunny

Bad Bunny, a Puerto Rican musician, is a pioneer in Latin trap and reggaeton. He gained fame with his unique style and infectious beats, and his popularity skyrocketed with hits like "Dakiti," "Mía," and "DÁKITI." His music and fashion challenge traditional gender norms, and he has become a cultural icon, influencing music and fashion worldwide

Olivia rodrigo

Olivia Rodrigo is an American singer-songwriter who gained fame with her debut single "drivers license." Her emotionally charged lyrics and pop-punk sound capture the trials and tribulations of youth. Rodrigo's debut album "SOUR" received critical acclaim, and she quickly established herself as a significant voice in the music industry.

Dua Lipa

Dua Lipa is an English singer-songwriter known for her powerful vocals and contemporary pop sound. She burst onto the scene with hits like "New Rules" and "Don't Start Now." Lipa's music often features disco and dance-pop elements, and she is recognized for her dynamic performances and strong visual aesthetics, making her one of the leading figures in modern pop.

Ariana Grande

Ariana Grande is an American singer and actress known for her powerful vocals and wide vocal range. She first gained fame as a Nickelodeon star before transitioning to music with hits like "The Way," "Thank U, Next," and "7 Rings." Grande's blend of pop, R&B, and trap, along with her distinct style, has made her one of the biggest pop stars in the world.

Lizzo

Lizzo is an American singer, rapper, and flutist who promotes body positivity and self-love through her music. With hits like "Truth Hurts," "Good as Hell," and "Juice," she combines elements of hip-hop, pop, and R&B, creating an empowering and infectious sound. Lizzo's charismatic personality and energy have garnered her a robust following.
