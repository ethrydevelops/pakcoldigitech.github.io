Lana Del Rey

About

Lana Del Rey is an American singer-songwriter known for her cinematic and melancholic style. Emerging on the music scene in 2011 with her breakout single "Video Games," she quickly gained acclaim for her distinctive voice and nostalgic, vintage-inspired sound. Her music often explores themes of love, fame, and the American Dream, and she’s celebrated for her unique aesthetic that blends elements of pop, rock, and indie. Lana Del Rey has received critical acclaim and numerous awards for her work, including Grammys, and continues to be a significant influence in contemporary music.

Discography

Her discography includes:

  • "Born to Die" (2012)
  • "Paradise" (2012)
  • "Ultraviolence" (2014)
  • "Honeymoon" (2015)
  • "Lust for Life" (2017)

  • "Chemtrails over the Country Club" (2021)
  • "Blue Banisters" (2021)
  • "Did You Know That There’s a Tunnel Under Ocean Blvd" (2023)
